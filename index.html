About Funyula

FUNYULA

Funyula Constituency is an electoral constituency in Kenya. It is one of seven constituencies in Busia County. The constituency was established for the 1997 elections. At independence it was known as Busia Central represented in parliament by the Late James Machio till 1967 upon his death. During the resulting By-elections the seat was won by the Late Habil Kanani who lost the seat to Arthur Ochwada in the General Elections of 1969. Ochwada served one term and lost to Dr. Julia Ojiambo in the General Election of 1974. Dr. Ojiambo served two terms and lost Moody Awori in the snap General Elections of 1983.

Funyula Constituency, nestled in the heart of Busia County, Kenya, is a vibrant tapestry of rich culture and tradition. Its diverse communities celebrate a multitude of festivals, showcasing traditional dances, music, and colorful attire.

The local cuisine, marked by fresh ingredients and unique recipes, offers a true taste of the region's heritage. Craftsmanship thrives here, with artisans producing intricate beadwork, pottery, and carvings that tell stories of the past.

The constituency is also home to historical landmarks and natural beauty, from the serene shores of Lake Victoria to lush landscapes. Funyula's culture is not just a relic of history but a living, breathing entity that continues to evolve, making it a captivating destination for anyone seeking to experience the wonders of Kenya’s cultural diversity.

HON. DR. OUNDO MUDENYO

M.P Funyula Constituency


Dr Wilberforce Oundo is the fourth Member of parliament for Funyula Constituency. Currently servung his second term on a Orange Democratic Movement (ODM). Dr Oundo began his eduaction in Bujwanga Primary then later on proceeded to secondary education in 1986 to 1989 where he joined Starehe Boys Centre & School. Between 1990 and 1995, he attended the University of Nairobi to pursue a Bachelor of Arts in Land Economics. He went back to University of Nairobi to take Masters of Land Economics from 1998 and 2002. Again in 2007, he was enrolled at the University of Nairobi for PhD in Land Economics till 2011. In 1995, Wilberforce Oundo got his first job at Kinyua Koech Ltd where he worked as a Trainee Valuer until 1997. From 1997 to 1998, he joined the Ryden Meghraj as a Property Manager/Valuer. Between 1999 and 2010, Wilberforce Oundo worked at Regent Group as a Chief Executive Officer. In 2010, he started Roack Consult Limited as a Director and from 2013 to 2017 he was a lecturer at the University of Nairobi. In 2017, he joined the politics and vied for parliamentarian seat where he won and became a Member of Parliament until now.
